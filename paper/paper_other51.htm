机群系统可信性评价的研究

摘要

论文在机群系统的可信性评价的模型、方法和工具方面进行了研究。
论文首先介绍了适合机群可信性评价的若干指标，对系统可用性、系统可靠性和任 务完成情况三种指标进行了形式化描述，并通过一个双节点机群的例子分析和比较了不 同指标的适用条件和衡量结果。

论文接着介绍了机群可信性建模的理论和方法，阐述了机群模型中故障速率，故障 覆盖率和恢复速率三种基本参数的语义，分析了它们的特征和估计方法；同时介绍了当 前可信性建模中广泛使用的可靠性框图、故障树、Markov 链和Petri 网模型，对这些模 型的描述能力和建模的复杂程度进行了分析和对比。

论文的主要工作是采用可靠性框图方法对机群的基本可信性进行评价，提出基于着 色Petri 网的高可用机群的可信性评价方法，开发了一个着色Petri 网的模拟工具NCPN 和高可用机群的可信性评价工具ACUTE，并研究了一个高可用机群系统的实例。

应用可靠性框图方法可对机群的基本可信性进行分析，论文以曙光4000A 机群系统 为实例，评价了其基本可信性指标，指出262 个节点的机群的MTTF 时间仅有一天。

论文提出了一种基于着色Petri 网的高可用机群的可信性评价方法，该方法使用着 色Petri 网理论，采用了层次化的建模方法设计了描述机群行为的子模型库，该模型库 支持当前主要的对等式和集中式高可用软件结构，采用了动态建模方法选择合适的子模 型搭建整体模型。

为了实际应用基于着色Petri 网的机群可信性模型，论文设计开发了一个着色
Petri 网的模拟工具NCPN；该工具采用了面向对象的设计机制进行模型设计，提供了基 本Petri 网的对象库作为建模的框架，实现了层次化建模的方法，提出了变量弧和禁止 弧的着色Petri 网结构扩展。基于着色Petri 网模型和NCPN 工具，论文开发了一个用 于高可用机群可信性评价的工具ACUTE，该工具根据用户的输入参数自动建立系统模型， 进行模型的模拟，生成系统可用性和任务完成情况等可信性指标。

使用ACUTE，论文还研究了一个高可用机群系统的实例，该系统由16 个节点组成， 运行计算型任务和Oracle 数据库服务两种应用的多个实例。机群和应用的参数由历史数据和简单的故障注入实验获得，针对不同情形下的评价结果，论文讨论了采用检查点 机制后的任务完成指标的提高，分析了各种参数对应用恢复策略选择的影响。论文初步 验证了机群可信性定性和定量评价的可行性和方法。

关键词：机群，可信性评价，着色Petri 网，层次化建模

 

Research of Dependability Evaluation of Cluster System
Liu Xinyu (Computer Architecture)
Directed by Sun Ninghui

Abstract

This thesis is focused on the research of the models, approaches and tools of dependability evaluation in computer cluster systems.

First, the paper introduces a suitable set of dependability measures for clusters, and describes the formulation of three classes of measures, including system availability, reliability and task completion. The applicability and results of different measures are given and compared through the analysis of a two-node cluster system.

Then the paper discusses the theories of cluster dependability modeling. The input parameters such as failure rates, coverage probabilities and recovery rates are investigated with respect to their characteristics and estimation methods. Reliability Block Diagram, Fault tree, Markov Chain and Petri Nets models, commonly used for dependability analysis, are reviewed and their modeling capability and solution complexity are discussed.

The main work of the paper is applying Relaibility Block Graph (RBD) to the evaluation of basic cluster dependability, presenting an approach for the dependability analysis of high-availability (HA) clusters, designing a simulation tool NCPN for Coloured Petri Nets (CPN) and a HA cluster dependability evaluation tool ACUTE, and finally studying an
instance of HA cluster.

Using RBD modeling, the basic dependability of clusters is analyzed. The paper chooses the DWANING 4000a cluster as instance and evaluates its basic dependability measures, which show that the MTTF of a 262-node cluster is only one day.

The paper presents an approach for the dependability analysis of HA clusters. Based on Coloured Petri Nets, the approach uses hierarchical modeling to design a submodel library for the characterization of cluster behaviors. The library supports major Peer-to-Peer and
Centralized HA cluster architecture, and forms the global model by dynamic modeling.

In order to solve the dependability models based on Coloured Petri Nets, a simulation tool called NCPN is developed. The object-oriented mechanism and event-driven simulation are applied in NCPN. A class library is provided to model a wide range of different structures
and behaviors of CPN. Benefits of NCPN such as hierarchical modeling and CPN structure extensions are mentioned.

To demonstrate the use of ACUTE, a detailed dependability analysis is carried out on a full-system example representative of HA clusters. The system is composed of sixteen nodes, and running several instances of a computing task and Oracle database service. The parameters are retrieved by history profiles and simple fault injection experiments. The paper discusses the improvement of task-completion measures due to checkpoint mechanism, andcasts new light on the influences of arameters upon the selection of application recovery strategies. The paper provides a preliminary validation of the applicability of the qualitative and quantitative evaluation of cluster dependability and discusses the approaches.

Key words: Cluster, Dependability Evaluation, Coloured Petri Nets, Hierarchical Modeling
